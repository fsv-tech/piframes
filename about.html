About · the eye behind the lens

Hi, I'm Chase. I chase light for a living.

PI Frames started with a shoebox of prints — the kind you dig out at family gatherings, pass around, and argue about lovingly. Screens come and go; a photograph you can hold stays. That's the whole idea behind the name: pictures worth framing.

I shoot weddings, love stories and portraits across South Africa, in both stills and film. My style leans warm and honest — I'd rather wait for a real laugh than pose a fake one, and I schedule sessions around the golden hour because that's when light is kindest to people.

On the day, I work quietly. The best frames happen when you forget I'm there: a squeezed hand during speeches, a grandmother's smile from the third row, the two of you sneaking out at sunset. Afterwards, every image is hand-finished — colour, light and skin tones tuned until the photo feels the way the moment felt.

If that sounds like your kind of storytelling, I'd love to hear what you're planning.
